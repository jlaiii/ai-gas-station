🧠 Why Shop Around?

🏪AI Providers Are Gas Stations

You wouldn't fill up your car at the most expensive station in town without checking prices. AI tokens are the same — different providers sell the same "fuel" (intelligence) at wildly different prices. The cheapest option is often 90% as good for most tasks.

🔧Match the Model to the Task

Writing a quick script? Use DeepSeek v4-flash ($0.28/M). Building a complex system with nuanced reasoning? Maybe Sonnet 4.6 ($15/M) is worth it. Most coding agents burn tokens on boilerplate, refactors, and debugging — tasks where the cheap model works just fine.

💸The "Default" Trap

People stick with Claude Code or Codex CLI because it's what they know. But the CLI you use is just the pump handle — you can swap the fuel behind it. Tools like OpenCode, Continue.dev, and Aider let you bring your own API key and choose any provider.

📊Real Talk: Benchmarks vs Reality

Benchmark scores don't tell the full story. A model scoring 2% lower on some eval might save you 90% on tokens. In practice, the difference between a $0.87/M model and a $30/M model is often just a few extra iterations — not a fundamentally different outcome.

🔄Multi-Provider Strategy

Smart developers use multiple providers: cheap models for boilerplate and iteration, premium models for architecture decisions and tricky bugs. Tools like OpenRouter and Hermes Agent make it easy to route to different models based on the task.
